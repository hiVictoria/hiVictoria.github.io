/ CONTEXT

The Current State: Account Favorites & Likes

Favorited Properties

As part of the Novemeber launch, users on the Homes.com native app can save properties by 'Favoriting' a property. These Favorites can be found in their account profile under 'More'

Liked Photos

In the case where users may not be interested in the property as a whole but reasonate with a particular photo from a part of the home, users can save specific photos by 'Liking' a photo

The Problem

Deep Scrolling

Currently the app aggregates all the user's favorited properties and liked photos into one place under their account profile. This leads to a deep scrolling issue when a user has 10, 15, 20+ items saved during their home buying process. As their saved items accumulate, searching and sifting through this list becomes tedious and painful - how can we improve this so that the homebuying process is more seamless?

Lack of User Customization

Users may want to have the option to self organize their favorited homes or liked photos but currently don’t have the ability/functionality to, leading much to be desired in tailoring their saved – . How can we allow users to customize their experience and organize their saved items in the way they want to?

The Idea

Give users the ability to self-organize and sort their
favorited homes/liked photos into boards/albums

Looking at Other Apps

Many other popular apps on the market use organizational lists to give users the functionality to save and group media, including Pinterest and Airbnb. In particular, Airbnb allows users to save listings to what they call 'Wishlists'. By default, wishlists are named based on the location.

Previous Research

Previous user research had been conducted in August 2021 by Anahita Sanandaji, PhD, gauging the user experience and flow of saving a property to account favorites. Feedback testimonials from the research supported that users wanted this type of orgnaizational feature.

“being able to save properties based on certain criteria.. or more advanced lists”

“just want the ability to add properties to lists and view the lists on seperate pages...”

/ RESEARCH

Before beginning the design process, I worked with Costar's UX Research (UXR) team to determine how users engage with the Favoriting feature and in what ways they would like to organize their saved properties.

Background

    Think aloud survey that asked users to engage with Homes.com's Favoriting feature on mobile web

    Note: Because Homes.com did not currently have Liked photos feature live in production, the initial survey focused on favorited properties. However, because the basic idea of saving a photo or property is the same and uses similar user flows, we assume that the results here are relevant for liked photos.

Goals

    Guage ease of use for favoriting property

    Guage navigational ability to find account Favorites

    Guage overall interest in organizational capability

Survey Results

Users struggled to find where to locate their account Favorites

While 100% of participants agreed that favoriting a property was ‘very easy’,

47% of users disagreed that the process of navigating to Favorites was not ‘very easy’

Users wanted to categorize homes they were interested in

Sort & Organize by

  • Price
  • # Bedrooms
  • Sq Footage
  • Location
