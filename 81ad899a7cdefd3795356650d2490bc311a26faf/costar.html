T'Lai Madindale (UX Research)

We all know the homebuying process is

  • stressful.
  • impersonal.
  • overwhelming.
  • daunting.

Prospective homeowners face a whirlwind of emotions and challenges when venturing into the homebuying market.

A Mobile-First Approach

93%

of buyers
use a mobile website or app
during their home search.

Research indicates that an increasing number of home shoppers use mobile apps and web during their home buying process. These homebuyers want to be able to easily save and organize their listings as they compare and share different properties with their agent and family friends.

The Current State: Account Favorites & Likes

Favorited Properties

Users on the native app can save properties by "Favoriting" a property, which can be found under their account profile.

Liked Photos

Users can save specific photos of a property by "Liking" a photo. These liked photos would also be found in their account favorites.

The Problem

Deep Scrolling

Currently, all of the user's favorited properties and liked photos are aggregated in their account profile. This leads to a deep scrolling issue when a user has 10, 15, 20+ items saved during their home buying process. As their saved items accumulate, searching and sifting through this list becomes tedious and painful.

Lack of User Customization

Users don't have any ability to self-organize their favorited homes or liked photos, leading to a frustrating experience when wanting to customize their management of saved items.

The Idea

Give users the ability to self-organize and sort their
favorited homes/liked photos into boards/albums

Looking at Other Apps

Many other popular apps on the market use organizational lists to give users the functionality to save and group media, including Pinterest and Airbnb. In particular, Airbnb allows users to save listings to 'Wishlists'. By giving users the ability to self-sort their media, users can more quickly find what they're looking for and are encouraged to continue browsing and saving items of interest.

Previous Research

Previous user research was conducted in August 2021 by Anahita Sanandaji, PhD, gauging the user experience and flow of saving a property to account favorites.
Testimonials and feedback from the existing research supported that users wanted this organizational feature.

“being able to save properties based on certain criteria.. or more advanced lists”

“just want the ability to add properties to lists and view the lists on seperate pages...”

/ RESEARCH

Before beginning the design process, I worked with Costar's UX Research (UXR) team to determine how users engage with the Favoriting feature and in what ways they evaluate and organize their saved properties.

Method

    Think aloud survey that asked users to engage with Homes.com's Favoriting feature on mobile web

Goals

    Measure ease of use for favoriting property

    Track navigational ability to find account Favorites

    Guage overall interest in organizational capability

What We Learned

Users struggled to find where to locate their account Favorites

While all participants agreed that favoriting a property was very easy,

47% of users disagreed, indicating that the process of navigating to Favorites was not intuitive

Users wanted to categorize homes they were interested in

Sort & Organize by

  • Price
  • # Bedrooms
  • Sq Footage
  • Location
